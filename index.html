Jesus Of Nazareth

"I am the way, the truth, and the life"

Here's a brief timeline about Jesus' life:

  • Birth in Bethlehem (c. 4-6 BC): Jesus is born to Mary and Joseph in Bethlehem, fulfilling Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah's birth.
  • Visit by the Wise Men (c. 4 BC): Magi from the East bring gifts to the young Jesus, recognizing Him as the King of the Jews.
  • Flight to Egypt (c. 4 BC): Jesus' family flees to Egypt to escape King Herod's decree to kill all young boys in Bethlehem.
  • Return to Nazareth (c. 4-1 BC): After Herod's death, Jesus and His family return and settle in Nazareth.
  • Jesus in the Temple (c. AD 8): At age 12, Jesus amazes teachers in the Temple with His understanding of the Scriptures.
  • Baptism by John the Baptist (c. AD 26-29): Jesus is baptized in the Jordan River, marking the beginning of His public ministry.
  • First Miracle at Cana (c. AD 27-30): Jesus turns water into wine at a wedding, performing His first recorded miracle.
  • Sermon on the Mount (c. AD 28-30): Jesus delivers one of His most famous teachings, emphasizing love, humility, and the Beatitudes.
  • Feeding the 5,000 (c. AD 28-30): Jesus miraculously feeds a large crowd with five loaves of bread and two fish.
  • Triumphal Entry into Jerusalem (c. AD 30-33): Jesus enters Jerusalem on a donkey, greeted by crowds waving palm branches.
  • Crucifixion (c. AD 30-33): Jesus is crucified, sacrificing Himself for the sins of humanity.
  • Resurrection (c. AD 30-33): Jesus rises from the dead three days after His crucifixion, appearing to His disciples and many others.
  • Ascension (c. AD 30-33): Jesus ascends to Heaven, promising to return and establish His Kingdom.

Fulfilled Prophecies in the Bible:

  • The Birth of Jesus in Bethlehem: "But you, Bethlehem Ephrathah... out of you will come for me one who will be ruler over Israel" (Micah 5:2, NIV). This prophecy was fulfilled in Matthew 2:1.
  • The Virgin Birth: "Behold, the virgin shall conceive and bear a son, and shall call his name Immanuel" (Isaiah 7:14, ESV). Fulfilled in Matthew 1:22-23.
  • The Crucifixion of Jesus: "They have pierced my hands and my feet" (Psalm 22:16, KJV). This prophecy describes the method of Jesus' death, fulfilled in the Gospels (John 19:18).
  • The Resurrection of Jesus: "For you will not abandon my soul to Sheol, or let your holy one see corruption" (Psalm 16:10, NASB). Fulfilled in Acts 2:31.
  • The Promise of the Holy Spirit: "I will pour out my Spirit on all people" (Joel 2:28, NLT). Fulfilled in Acts 2:1-4 during Pentecost.
  • The Suffering Servant: "He was despised and rejected by mankind, a man of suffering, and familiar with pain" (Isaiah 53:3, NIV). This describes Jesus' life and crucifixion, fulfilled in the Gospels.
  • The Fall of Jerusalem: Jesus predicted, "Not one stone here will be left on another; every one will be thrown down" (Matthew 24:2, NIV). This prophecy was fulfilled in AD 70.
  • The Spread of the Gospel: "You will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth" (Acts 1:8, ESV). This promise continues to be fulfilled through global missionary work.
  • Promise of Eternal Life: "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life" (John 3:16, ESV). This promise is fulfilled in the lives of believers.

Evidence of the Bible’s Reliability:

  • Historical Accuracy: Archaeological discoveries, such as the Dead Sea Scrolls and the ancient city of Jericho, confirm many events, places, and people described in the Bible. (Source: "Archaeology and the Bible" by John McRay)
  • Manuscript Evidence: The Bible has more manuscript copies than any other ancient text, with over 5,000 Greek New Testament manuscripts, ensuring the text has been accurately preserved over time. (Source: "The Text of the New Testament" by Bruce M. Metzger and Bart D. Ehrman)
  • Fulfilled Prophecies: The Bible contains hundreds of prophecies that have been fulfilled, such as the birth of Jesus in Bethlehem, His crucifixion, and the fall of Jerusalem. (Source: "Evidence That Demands a Verdict" by Josh McDowell)
  • Consistency Across Authors: Despite being written by over 40 different authors over 1,500 years, the Bible maintains a consistent message of God’s love, redemption, and plan for humanity. (Source: "The Case for Christ" by Lee Strobel)
  • Scientific Insights: While not a science textbook, the Bible describes certain scientific principles (like the Earth being round) that align with modern discoveries long before they were widely known. (Source: "The Genesis of Science" by James Hannam)
  • Archaeological Discoveries: Sites like the Pool of Bethesda, King David’s Palace, and the Pilate Stone validate biblical accounts and affirm the Bible’s historical credibility. (Source: "The Stones Cry Out" by Randall Price)
  • Impact on Lives: The transformative power of the Bible in individuals' lives across centuries testifies to its reliability and divine inspiration. (Source: "Mere Christianity" by C.S. Lewis)
  • Textual Integrity: Rigorous methods of textual criticism have demonstrated that the Bible’s text is substantially pure and has been transmitted with high accuracy. (Source: "The New Testament Documents: Are They Reliable?" by F.F. Bruce)
